I'd add to this that EBX had no pre-mine or ICO. All EBX that Ryan has sold was earned through operating a mine or mining. At the current token price (10 cents), Ryan is unwilling to sell the EBX that he worked for because he thinks EBX is worth more than that so he is looking for other revenue streams.

Keep in mind that EBX has a 42 million supply, so this price point is conceptually equivalent to a 20 cent token price on bitcoin or any bitcoin fork which has a 21 million supply.

In this regard, Ryan's costs are incredibly modest. For a developer in America, $5,000 per month for both cost of living and business expenses is very low.

If the project is successful long-term, anyone here today has a fair opportunity to share in that success either by either mining EBX or acquiring EBX from someone else who mined them. Just by being here, YOU are the early backer -- unlike with many memecoins, there is no earlier investor than whoever chooses to be here now. If Ryan succeeds, you succeed. 

Undeniably, being an early backer or developer of a new project is risky, but Ryan's clearly running on a shoestring budget at this time. Nevertheless, he isn't compromising on his ideals for how a blockchain should be designed, distributed, and funded (which he has modelled much closer to bitcoin than many other cryptocurrency projects).
